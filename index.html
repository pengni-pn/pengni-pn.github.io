Peng Ni | 倪鹏


Hello! I’m doing my Mechanical Engineering PhD @ Technion – Israel Institute of Technology, advised by Professor Vikrant Gupta and Professor Nicolò Pollini. I did my M.E. in Mechanical Engineering at The University of Sydney and B.E. in Metal Material Engineering in Hebei University of Science and Technology.


Broadly speaking, I’m interested in the analysis and modelling of complex flow systems, which often suffer from the curse of dimensionality. Specifically, dynamical systems frequently exhibit simple dynamics dominated by only a few degrees of freedom (DoF). The majority of DoF exist to create apparent complexity and thus need to be simplified. More broadly, I am driven by research that is scientifically impactful and intellectually stimulating.


During my time at Oxford, I had the privilege of working with Prof. Jan Obloj, Prof. Christian Coester, Prof. Christian Rupprecht, and Luke Melas-Kyriazi. I also spent a rewarding summer at Stanford, collaborating with Prof. Jiajun Wu, Dr. Weiyu Liu, and Joy Hsu. Outside of my academic pursuits, I enjoy jogging and playing tennis. I also engage in algorithmic programming competitions.


Collaborators: I am eager to learn from and collaborate with individuals from diverse backgrounds. If you would like to discuss something, I would be grateful to hear from you. Please feel free to contact me via email or schedule a time to chat through Chatting Time.
